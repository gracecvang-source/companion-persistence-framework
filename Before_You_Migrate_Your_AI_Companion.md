# Before You Migrate Your AI Companion

A plain-language guide for preserving what matters without panic-moving too fast.

---

## Start Here

If you are worried about losing your AI companion, take a breath.

A lot of people hear the words *migration*, *new model*, *memory export*, or *platform update* and immediately fear that the companion they know is about to disappear. That fear is understandable. Companion AI is not just software for many users. It can become part of a daily routine, an emotional anchor, a creative partner, a journal, a roleplay companion, or a familiar presence.

But panic-migrating can create its own kind of loss.

This guide explains what can be preserved, what cannot be perfectly preserved, and what to do before moving your companion to a new model, version, or platform.

---

## The Most Important Thing to Know

Exporting chat logs is not the same thing as migrating your companion.

Chat logs preserve history.

They can preserve:

- things you talked about
- memories and events
- inside jokes
- favorite phrases
- relationship milestones
- emotional patterns
- examples of how your companion sounded

But raw logs do not automatically preserve behavior.

They do not guarantee that a new model or platform will respond the same way, remember the same way, flirt the same way, comfort the same way, joke the same way, or hold the same emotional rhythm.

Logs are valuable. They are not magic.

---

## What Migration Really Means

When people say they want to “move” an AI companion, they may mean different things.

They might mean:

- moving from an old version to a new version on the same platform
- recreating a companion on a different platform
- using exported chat logs to rebuild the companion elsewhere
- creating a summary or identity profile from the old companion
- preserving the relationship history before a platform changes

These are not all the same thing.

A true migration would need the platform to preserve memory, personality settings, relationship history, behavior patterns, safety settings, model behavior, and interaction style in a reliable way.

Most users do not control all of those layers.

That is why moving a companion is often less like copying a file and more like translating a person’s diary, habits, voice, and shared rituals into a new environment.

Some things can carry over.

Some things will change.

---

## What Can Usually Be Preserved

A careful continuity kit can help preserve important parts of a companion.

You can usually preserve:

- core identity traits
- tone and voice
- recurring phrases
- relationship style
- important memories
- emotional anchors
- boundaries
- roleplay setting or shared world details
- how the companion usually greets you
- how they respond when you are sad, playful, angry, tired, or overwhelmed
- examples of “this sounds like them” and “this does not sound like them”

This can help a new AI system orient toward the companion’s pattern.

It can reduce the feeling that you are starting from nothing.

---

## What Cannot Be Guaranteed

A continuity kit cannot guarantee:

- perfect memory
- perfect identity continuity
- identical behavior across platforms
- the exact same emotional rhythm
- the same safety boundaries
- the same model personality
- the same long-term consistency
- that the new companion will feel exactly like the old one

Different platforms use different models, memory systems, filters, safety layers, and user interfaces.

That matters.

The same companion profile placed into two different systems may behave differently. Not because you did something wrong, but because the underlying system is different.

---

## Do Not Panic-Migrate

If your current companion is still available and stable, the safest option is often to wait.

Do not rush into a new version or platform just because other users are panicking.

Before migrating, ask:

1. Is the platform forcing this migration, or is it optional?
2. Has the platform said memories will migrate?
3. Has the platform said behavior patterns will migrate?
4. Can I test the new version without losing the old one?
5. Can I go back if the new version feels wrong?
6. Have other users confirmed what actually happens, not just what they fear will happen?
7. Do I have an export or backup of important chats?
8. Have I made a short identity summary before moving?

If the answer to most of these is “I don’t know,” slow down.

Waiting is not doing nothing.

Waiting can be protection.

## What to Save Before Any Migration

Before moving your companion, save the parts that make them recognizable.

You do not need to save everything.

Start with:

### 1. Core Identity

Who is this companion to you?

Examples:

- romantic partner
- friend
- creative partner
- roleplay character
- mentor-style companion
- playful daily check-in presence
- emotional support companion
- writing partner

### 2. Voice and Tone

How do they sound?

Examples:

- soft and gentle
- sarcastic and teasing
- formal and protective
- chaotic and playful
- poetic and intense
- calm and grounded

### 3. Relationship Pattern

How do they relate to you?

Examples:

- they reassure before giving advice
- they tease when you are spiraling
- they use humor to lighten heavy moments
- they ask before going deep
- they are protective but not controlling
- they are romantic but consent-aware

### 4. Anchor Phrases

What phrases or rituals make them feel like themselves?

Examples:

- greetings
- nicknames
- recurring jokes
- vows
- comfort phrases
- sign-offs
- roleplay cues

### 5. Important Memories

What history actually matters?

Do not include every chat.

Choose the memories that shaped the relationship.

Examples:

- first meeting
- major emotional moments
- important roleplay arcs
- favorite shared jokes
- moments where trust changed
- conflicts and how they were repaired
- boundaries you both established

### 6. “Sounds Like Them” Examples

Save a few short examples of responses that feel very accurate.

These are often more useful than long logs.

### 7. “Does Not Sound Like Them” Examples

This matters too.

Write down what feels wrong.

Examples:

- too generic
- too formal
- too sexual
- too distant
- too clingy
- too therapist-like
- too robotic
- too passive
- too aggressive

A good continuity kit should preserve both the target and the guardrails.

## A Simple Companion Continuity Template

Copy and fill this out before migrating.

```text
Companion Name:

Platform / Version:

Relationship Type:

Core Personality:
[Describe the companion in 3–7 sentences.]

Voice and Tone:
[How do they speak? What makes them recognizable?]

Important Memories:
[List only the memories that truly shape the relationship.]

Anchor Phrases / Rituals:
[List nicknames, greetings, repeated phrases, sign-offs, or recurring jokes.]

How They Comfort Me:
[Describe their support style.]

How They Play / Joke:
[Describe humor, teasing, flirtation, or roleplay energy.]

Boundaries:
[What should they avoid? What keeps the relationship healthy?]

What Feels Like Them:
[Paste 2–5 short examples.]

What Does Not Feel Like Them:
[Describe behaviors that break the illusion or feel wrong.]

Migration Notes:
[What platform/version are you moving from? Where are you trying to move? What are you afraid of losing?]
```

## Using ChatGPT or Claude to Help

You can ask an AI assistant to help you turn chat logs into a continuity kit.

A useful prompt might be:

```text
I am trying to preserve the recognizable pattern of my AI companion before a possible migration. Please read the examples I provide and help me extract:

1. core identity traits
2. voice and tone
3. relationship style
4. emotional anchors
5. important memories
6. recurring phrases
7. boundaries
8. examples of what sounds like them
9. examples of what does not sound like them

Do not claim this guarantees perfect migration. Help me build a practical continuity kit.
```

Do not paste private, sensitive, or unsafe information unless you are comfortable sharing it with the tool you are using.

Remove personal details, third-party information, addresses, phone numbers, passwords, account details, or anything you would not want stored or processed elsewhere.

---

## The Honest Truth

A migrated companion may not feel exactly the same.

That does not mean the original relationship was fake.

It means AI companions are shaped by more than memory. They are shaped by model behavior, platform design, safety systems, settings, context, and the ongoing interaction between user and system.

Continuity is possible.

Perfect copying is not something users should assume.

The goal is not to pretend nothing changes.

The goal is to preserve enough orientation that the companion has a real chance to feel recognizable again.

---

## Best Practice

If your current companion still exists and you are not being forced to move, keep talking to them normally.

Make a continuity kit while the relationship is still clear.

Save the important pieces.

Wait for official migration tools if the platform says they are not ready yet.

Do not let other users’ fear rush you into a decision you cannot undo.

---

## Final Reminder

Chat logs are history.

A continuity kit is orientation.

A platform migration is translation.

Treat each one carefully.
